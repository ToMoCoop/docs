## Dispatch By Output Quantity setting

This setting controls how dispatch tasks are created for products made from a Bill of Materials (BOM) when **Assemble on Pick** is enabled. It determines whether the system creates one dispatch task per pick task, or one per individual component.

## How it works

| Setting | Behaviour | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Off** (recommended) | One dispatch task per pick task | Large dispatches where detailed component tracking is not needed |
| **On** | One dispatch task per individual component for each assembled kit | Detailed tracking and auditing, especially when components are shipped separately |

<Info>This setting does not affect pick tasks -- it only changes the number of dispatch tasks created.</Info>

## Example: Xbox and Cyberpunk 2077 Bundle

Imagine you have 10 Xboxes and 10 copies of Cyberpunk 2077. Your BOM product is an "Xbox and Cyberpunk 2077 Bundle" (one of each, output quantity: 1, Assemble on Pick is on). A sales order for 10 bundles creates 2 pick tasks.

**When off:** 1 dispatch task for Xbox + 1 dispatch task for Cyberpunk 2077 = **2 total**.

**When on:** 10 dispatch tasks for Xbox + 10 dispatch tasks for Cyberpunk 2077 = **20 total**.

## Example: Tin Can Telephone

Your BOM product requires 1 Wire and 2 Tin Cans (output quantity: 1). A sales order for 10 telephones creates 3 pick tasks.

**When off:** 1 dispatch task for Wire + 2 dispatch tasks for Tin Can = **3 total**.

**When on:** 10 dispatch tasks for Wire (qty 1 each) + 10 dispatch tasks for Tin Can (qty 2 each) = **20 total**.

<Accordion title="FAQ">
  **Can I change this setting at any time?**

  Yes, you can toggle it on or off depending on your operational requirements.

  **When should I turn this setting on?**

  Turn it on when you need detailed tracking and the ability to audit each individual component being dispatched, especially if components are shipped separately.
</Accordion>
