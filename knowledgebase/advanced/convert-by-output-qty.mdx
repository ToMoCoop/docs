## Convert By Output Quantity setting

This setting controls how conversion tasks are generated when you process works orders for products made from a Bill of Materials (BOM). You can find it in the **Conversion** section of your account settings.

## How it works

| Setting | Behaviour | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Off** | One conversion task per pick task | Bulk conversions without detailed individual tracking |
| **On** (recommended) | One conversion task per child product per job | Detailed stock tracking and full auditability |

<Info>This setting does not affect pick tasks -- it only changes the number of conversion tasks created.</Info>

## Example: Xbox and Cyberpunk 2077 Bundle

Your BOM product requires one Xbox and one Cyberpunk 2077 (output quantity: 1). A works order for 10 bundles creates 2 pick tasks.

**When off:** 1 conversion task for Xbox + 1 for Cyberpunk 2077 = **2 total**.

**When on:** 10 conversion tasks for Xbox + 10 for Cyberpunk 2077 = **20 total**.

## Example: Tin Can Telephone

Your BOM product requires 1 Wire and 2 Tin Cans (output quantity: 1). A works order for 10 telephones creates 3 pick tasks.

**When off:** 1 conversion task for Wire + 2 for Tin Can = **3 total**.

**When on:** 10 conversion tasks for Wire (qty 1 each) + 10 for Tin Can (qty 2 each) = **20 total**.

<Accordion title="FAQ">
  **What is the recommended setting?**

  **On** is recommended for maximum auditability, as it tracks each child product conversion in detail.

  **Can I switch between the two settings?**

  Yes, you can toggle the setting on or off depending on how much detail you need.

  **Does the setting affect pick tasks?**

  No, it only affects the number of conversion tasks created.
</Accordion>
