The warehouse map provides a visual overview of your warehouse, showing zones, aisles, and stock statuses. Before you can use it, your locations need to be configured with the correct zone and dimensional information.

<Steps>
<Step title="Configure your locations">
Go to the **Locations** menu. Select the location you want to edit, then scroll down to the **Details** tab. Input all the required zone and aisle information and click **Save**. This makes the location ready for the warehouse map.
</Step>
<Step title="Access the warehouse map">
Navigate to the **Stock** tab to open the warehouse map. The map shows zones and aisles, colour-coded by stock status (partial, full, or available). You can switch between warehouses using the **Warehouse** tab and filter for specific products using the **Find Product** tab.
</Step>
<Step title="View location details">
Click on any location on the map to see all the pallets and stock stored within it.
</Step>
<Step title="Use the 3D view">
For a more detailed view, switch to the 3D map. In 3D mode, you can zoom in, move the view with the left mouse button, and reposition with the right mouse button. Click on specific locations to see stock details, just like the 2D map.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
Always save your changes after configuring locations to ensure they appear correctly on the warehouse map.
</Info>

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="Why do my locations not appear on the warehouse map?">
Locations need to be configured with aisle or zone information and dimensional details before they can appear on the warehouse map. Make sure you have completed the location configuration step.
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="How can I filter locations on the warehouse map?">
You can apply filters to show locations containing specific products by using the **Find Product** tab on the warehouse map.
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Can I see stock details in the 3D view?">
Yes, the 3D view functions like the 2D warehouse map, allowing you to click on specific locations to see the stock details within.
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
