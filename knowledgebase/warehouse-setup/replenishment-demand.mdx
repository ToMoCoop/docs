**Demand replenishment** is a reactive process that replenishes pickface locations only when a pick task requires stock that is not available in any other pickface. Unlike continuous replenishment, which proactively keeps pickfaces stocked, demand replenishment responds directly to an immediate picking need.

## How demand replenishment works

1. **A pick task is generated.** The system first attempts to fulfil it using stock in the assigned continuous pickface locations.

2. **Stock is checked.** If continuous pickfaces have the required stock (including shelf-life criteria), the pick proceeds normally. If not, the system checks whether a demand replenishment location is enabled.

3. **Demand replenishment is triggered.** The pick task goes on hold while the system creates a replenishment task to move a full pallet of stock to an empty demand pickface. If no empty demand pickface is available, the replenishment task is also placed on hold until one becomes available.

4. **The pick task is released.** Once the replenishment is completed, the pick task is released from hold and proceeds as normal.

## Setting up demand pickfaces

<Steps>
<Step title="Navigate to the product">
From the left-hand menu, click **Products** and select the product you want to configure.
</Step>
<Step title="Assign locations">
Open the **Pickface** panel and click **New** to add a pickface location. You can optionally use **Location Groups** to manage multiple pickfaces simultaneously.

## Using continuous and demand replenishment together

The **Continuous** and **Demand** settings can be used simultaneously for the same product by assigning them to different pickface locations:

- **Primary Pickface (Continuous)**: Regularly replenished, holding the majority of stock
- **Secondary Pickface (Demand)**: Used when the primary cannot meet a specific order requirement (e.g., shelf-life dates)

**Example**: Product 123 has a customer requiring a maximum sell-by date of 10 days. The continuous pickface holds stock with 15 days remaining, which does not meet the requirement. The system places the pick on hold, generates a demand replenishment task to move stock with 5 days remaining from racking into the demand pickface, and then releases the pick task.

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="Why is my pick task on hold?">
The system is waiting for stock to be replenished into a pickface. If only continuous replenishment is enabled, the task stays on hold until a replenishment to a continuous pickface provides suitable stock. If demand replenishment is also enabled, a replenishment task is created to move stock into the demand pickface.
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="What if my demand replenishment pickface is not empty?">
The pick task and its associated replenishment task will be placed on hold until the demand pickface location is empty (quantity reaches 0).
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Do pickfaces hard-allocate stock?">
Demand replenishment pickfaces do hard-allocate the stock needed for picking, since a full pallet of specific stock must be moved into the pickface. Continuous replenishment pickfaces use soft allocation as standard.
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Where can I see the status of my pick and replenishment tasks?">
- **Picking List**: Add the Status column to the data grid to see the current status (e.g., On Hold)
- **Tasks**: Locate the task in the Tasks data grid and check the Status column. Hovering over the status shows the reason for any hold.
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
